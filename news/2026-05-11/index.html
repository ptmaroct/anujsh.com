Hardware Attestation as Monopoly Enabler

Attestation was sold as anti-fraud; it's quietly becoming the kill switch for everything Google doesn't own.

GrapheneOS lays out, in a single thread, what Play Integrity actually does once it becomes the default trust signal on Android: it lets a handful of OEMs and Google decide which OS, which browser, which build, which keys count as 'real'. The argument isn't paranoid — it's mechanical. Once banks, ride-hailing, government apps, and even mundane utilities key off remote attestation, anything that isn't a blessed stock image gets soft-bricked from the network, not from the device. Custom ROMs, dev builds, security-hardened forks like GrapheneOS itself, and any future competing OS all collapse into the same 'unverified' bucket.

What makes this the lead is the direction of travel: Apple already does this on the App Store side, the EU's eIDAS wallets are starting to require it, and Chrome's Web Environment Integrity rhymes uncomfortably. The piece is short, technical, and worth reading before the next time someone tells you attestation is 'just about fraud'. It is, until it isn't.
