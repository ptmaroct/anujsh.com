An OpenAI model has disproved a central conjecture in discrete geometry.

The headline isn't 'AI did math' — it's that the counterexample is short enough that humans can read and verify it.

OpenAI says one of its reasoning models found a counterexample to a long-standing open conjecture in discrete geometry, and the construction has been independently checked by mathematicians. The result itself is niche; what's interesting is the work pattern — humans steered, the model searched a space too tedious to brute-force by hand, and produced something tractable enough to audit.

Expect the 'frontier model as research assistant' framing to harden after this. The honest question isn't whether AI will do mathematics, but which results we'll trust without a human author and which we won't.
