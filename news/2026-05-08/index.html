Cloudflare to cut about 20% of workforce

When the company that brags about being the internet's plumbing trims a fifth of its staff, you're not watching cost discipline — you're watching the shape of the next decade.

Cloudflare is not a struggling business. Revenue is up, the stock is fine, Workers and AI inference sit at the centre of their pitch. Cutting 20% in that posture is a statement about what the company thinks engineering will look like in 2027 — leaner teams, more agentic tooling, fewer humans per service. If the most automation-forward infra company on the public markets is doing this when growth is healthy, expect every CFO with a board deck to read it as permission.

The second-order story is the labour pipeline. We just spent two years telling junior engineers that AI would augment them, not replace them. A 20% cut at a tier-one infra shop is the loudest counter-signal of the cycle. Watch what happens to compensation bands at the SRE/platform tier over the next two quarters — that's where the real argument gets settled.
