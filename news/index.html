Startup founders urge the U.S. not to shut off Chinese open-weight AI

Open weights are becoming infrastructure, and treating them as a switch to flip is a category mistake.

A group of startup founders is urging the U.S. government not to block access to Chinese open-weight AI models. The argument is not just ideological. Companies are already building products, research workflows and evaluation stacks around models that can be inspected and run independently.

The useful question is whether policy can confront real security concerns without confusing model access with deployment control. Once weights circulate, the durable advantage shifts to chips, data, talent and product execution anyway.
