Project Glasswing: Anthropic's first real interpretability update on a frontier model.

If 'we can read what the model is thinking' becomes a deployable feature rather than a research promise, half the AI-safety debate gets a different vocabulary overnight.

Glasswing is Anthropic's effort to turn mechanistic interpretability from circuit-by-circuit research into something usable on a deployed model. The update spells out, in unusually plain language for the lab, what's been mapped: the model's representations of refusal, of deception, of its own confidence — and what they were able to do with that map (flagging unfaithful chains of thought before the answer was returned). The deltas aren't huge in absolute terms, but they are the first time Anthropic has shown the technique scaling past toy settings.

The HN thread is where the texture lives. Researchers compare notes with the Apollo and DeepMind interpretability teams; ex-employees push back on what's been left out; a thoughtful sub-thread asks whether 'interpretability as product feature' is a category error. Worth reading not because it settles anything but because it is the clearest snapshot we have of where the alignment field's frontier actually sits in mid-2026.

    Why Japanese companies do so many different things.

    The memory shortage is causing a repricing of consumer electronics.

    DDR5 contract prices are up roughly 70% year-over-year and NAND is following; Tom's walks through how that's now bleeding into the retail price of laptops, phones, and SSDs in ways consumers can actually see. The proximate cause is AI server demand sucking up wafer allocations; the structural cause is that the memory cartel never really left. Expect another six to nine months of this before new fabs come online.

    Microsoft starts cancelling Claude Code licenses.

    Indexing a year of video locally on a 2021 MacBook with Gemma4-31B (50GB swap).

    Show HN: Freenet — a peer-to-peer platform for decentralised apps.

    Not the old Freenet — a new, ground-up rewrite with a clean Rust core, a contract-based key/value layer, and apps that run in the browser against a P2P backend. The interesting design choice is funding: the team has been heads-down for years without a token, and the launch post is explicit that there won't be one. The demo apps (a chat, a microblog, a forum) all worked when I tried them; whether it survives contact with real load is the next question.

    AI has a multiplying effect on existing technical skills.

    A small-but-careful internal study from a consultancy comparing the productivity bump that LLM tooling gave their senior versus junior engineers across six months of real client work.
