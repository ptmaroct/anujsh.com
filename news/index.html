Postmortem: TanStack NPM supply-chain compromise

If your dep graph touches React, you spent yesterday auditing — and most of us got off easy because the attacker was lazy.

TanStack's writeup walks through a textbook npm token compromise: maintainer creds phished, malicious versions of widely-used packages pushed, the npm 2FA story exposed yet again as enforcement-theatre on publish. The blast radius is wide — query, table, router, form — which is half the modern React stack. The interesting parts are the timeline (under three hours from publish to detection) and the candid bit about what would have happened if the payload had been quieter.

Read it as a checklist, not a war story. Pin versions, use npm provenance, scope your CI tokens, kill long-lived publishing creds. The next attacker won't fat-finger the obfuscation, and the JS ecosystem still rewards trusting strangers by default.
