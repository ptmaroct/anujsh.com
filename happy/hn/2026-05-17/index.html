A 0-click exploit chain for the Pixel 10

Google's flagship phone, owned with no user interaction — and the report came from inside the house.

Project Zero published a full chain that lets an attacker take over a stock Pixel 10 with zero clicks: a malformed message lands, parsers downstream make a series of bad assumptions, and an unsigned payload ends up executing in the radio process. From there, escalation to system is short. The writeup walks through the bug class — confused-deputy parsing in the cellular stack — that has now produced 0-clicks on three vendors in 18 months.

The interesting part is not that Pixel had a bug; everything has bugs. It is that Project Zero, sitting inside Google, is publicly auditing Google's hardware on the same terms as everyone else. That posture — even when uncomfortable — is the single most useful thing the industry can borrow.

    Bun's Rust rewrite ships with miri-failing UB in safe code
