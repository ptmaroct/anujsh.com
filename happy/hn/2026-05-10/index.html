A recent experience with ChatGPT 5.5 Pro

When the mathematician who proved theorems at age 35 finally bothers to test the model, the result is the only AI evaluation worth reading this week.

Tim Gowers — Fields Medalist, hard-to-impress, has been politely skeptical of LLM math for years — sat down with ChatGPT 5.5 Pro on real research-adjacent problems and writes down what happened. The post is unusual because it's specific: which problems, where the model got stuck, where it surprised him, what it does that no undergrad would. He doesn't reach for hype words, which is exactly why this lands harder than any benchmark thread.

The takeaway most people will quote is that the model is now a serious working partner for parts of mathematics — not a finished mathematician, but past the line where ignoring it is a defensible position. The 474-comment thread is split between mathematicians stress-testing his prompts and engineers reading too much into a single anecdote. Read the post, skip the comments. This is the AI evaluation that will still matter on Friday.
