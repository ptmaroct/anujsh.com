Mozilla to UK regulators: VPNs are essential privacy and security tools

A browser vendor stops being polite and starts naming the regulator's mistake out loud — exactly the role we keep saying Mozilla doesn't play any more.

Ofcom and the Home Office have been drifting toward treating consumer VPNs as a circumvention problem rather than a privacy tool. Mozilla's submission, public and unusually blunt, lays out the obvious: VPNs are how working journalists, abuse survivors, queer kids on family Wi-Fi, and bog-standard remote employees stay safe online. Hobbling them would not 'help with online safety'; it would lower the baseline of safety for everyone the regulator says it cares about.

The interesting part is the posture. Mozilla has spent two years being polite to governments that increasingly want to break the internet's privacy primitives. This submission is the first time in a while you can read it as a vendor saying, in print, that a regulator has misunderstood the problem. That tone is more useful than the document itself.
