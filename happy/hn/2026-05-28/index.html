I'm Tired of Talking to AI

The most-upvoted post on HN in months isn't a benchmark, a model, or a launch — it's a personal essay about wanting to read a human again.

An understated personal essay went nuclear: 1,838 points, ~900 comments, and the kind of agreement that usually only forms around outages. The author isn't anti-AI — they use it daily — they're just done with the texture of AI-generated answers everywhere, the hedging, the rounded-off tone, the sense that nobody is actually on the other side of the thread.

What makes the thread interesting isn't the original take (you've read variants of it) but the audience. The HN crowd is not the typical 'AI bad' demographic; this is the people shipping AI features. When even the builders are publicly admitting fatigue with their own output medium, that's a useful signal — somewhere between 'product problem' and 'category-level taste correction'. Worth bookmarking and rereading in six months.
