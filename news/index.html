Startup founders urge U.S. government not to shut off Chinese open-weight AI

Open weights are no longer just a research preference; they are becoming a trade-policy fault line.

The argument over Chinese open-weight models has escaped the lab. If governments treat access as a switch to flip, startups that depend on comparative evaluation, fine-tuning and model choice inherit the uncertainty first.

There is a real security case for limits, but blunt restrictions have a predictable side effect: they concentrate AI capability in the few vendors and governments large enough to absorb compliance. That is not a neutral market outcome.
