AI is breaking two vulnerability cultures

Disclosure norms were tuned for human researchers; LLM-shaped fuzzers don't read CONTRIBUTING.md.

Jeff Kaufman argues responsible disclosure depended on a balance: a small number of skilled researchers, a slow review pipeline, and humans on both sides who understood reputational stakes. AI-assisted vuln hunting collapses every assumption in that stack — volume goes up by orders of magnitude, the operators are anonymous bots, and the patch side can't keep pace with intake.

The piece is sharper than the usual 'AI changes security' takes because it names the cultures specifically: bug-bounty triage on one end, OSS maintainer culture on the other, both bleeding from the same wound. Worth reading slowly — this is the framing other people will quote next quarter.
