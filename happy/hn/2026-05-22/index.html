Google is testing new ad formats in Search and expanding its 'Direct Offers' pilot.

If 'Direct Offers' becomes the next AdWords, the search box quietly stops being a question — it becomes a checkout.

On the surface this is a marketing-team blog post: shoppable ad units inside AI Overviews, more vertical-specific creatives, a wider rollout of merchant-funded discount listings. Read it twice and the shape is clearer — Google is wiring transactions directly into the slot where the answer used to live. 'Search' is being slowly redefined as 'a place a wallet sits.'

The deeper anxiety in the 511-comment thread is the obvious one: as AI Overviews absorb more clicks and ads colonise more of the result page, the open web's referral economy thins by another notch. Every publisher who relied on Google's outbound traffic now has to plan for a world where Google's monetisation no longer requires them at all.
