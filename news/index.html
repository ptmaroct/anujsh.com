Removing the modem and GPS from my 2024 RAV4 hybrid

The car shipped with a cellular bill the owner never agreed to pay — the only fix the manufacturer offers is a soldering iron.

A meticulous teardown of how a stock 2024 Toyota now phones home, complete with a step-by-step recipe for unplugging it without bricking the infotainment system. The interesting part isn't the hack — it's the structural assumption underneath it: that a piece of hardware you bought outright still owes its manufacturer a constant connection, and that opting out is a hardware modification rather than a checkbox.

The comment thread is the other artefact. Hundreds of owners describe stumbling onto cellular fees, ambient location pings, and infotainment lockouts they only discovered after taking delivery. The post will get a lot of attention because the author makes the surgery legible; the broader story is that this kind of write-up is now a genre.
